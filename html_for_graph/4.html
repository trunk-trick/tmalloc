Fig. 2. The LIFO pop mechanism during a fastbin chunk allocation. In State A, the fastbin index points to the most recently freed chunk (the victim). When a memory request matches this bin size, malloc unlinks the victim by updating the fastbinsY[idx] head pointer to the victim->fd pointer. State B illustrates the updated linked list structure, where next becomes the new top of the bin.
